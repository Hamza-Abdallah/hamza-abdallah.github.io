    Urban Heat Island - UHI

    Analysis & Design

    Urban Heat Island (UHI) above the city of Banha, Egypt

    When your weather app tells you it's going to be a hot day, you might think it'll be a hot day for everyone in your city. But street configuration, building materials, vegetation, and closeness to a body of water significantly impact how city residents experience the weather. A hot and sunny afternoon in a neighborhood wrapped in mature trees and with canopy cover may feel quite pleasant. But a street surrounded by parking lots and concrete apartment buildings could be scorching.

    This project proposes to explore the possibility of using publicly available satellite imagery to monitor changes in temperature inside and outside the city by analyzing heat band 10 from Landsat 8 - OLI satellites in USGS Earth Explorer and accessing the air temperature inside the city to add many more parks and trees in the future to reduce altitude increasing in temperature.

    Human Field Study

    The human field study for Cairo University

    Cartography & Reports

    human field studies are used to understand how people interact with and are affected by their physical environment. This type of research is particularly useful for understanding issues such as urbanization, land use and land cover change, environmental degradation, and sustainability.

    A human field study in geography typically involves studying a specific area or region in order to understand the ways in which people are using the land, as well as the social, economic, and cultural factors that influence their use of the environment. Researchers may use a variety of methods in a human field study in geography, including Participant observation, Interviews, Surveys, and using GIS to generate maps.

    My tasks were :

  • Maps of land use and internal structure inside the campus of Cairo University
  • Road map inside the Cairo University campus
  • Distribution of samples (questionnaires) by colleges, students, and teams within the campus
  • Take notes on digging areas and water manholes inside the campus

    California Flood

    Raster Analysis & Cartography

    Flood of January 1997 in the Lake Tahoe Basin, California and Nevada

    Northern California and western Nevada, including the Lake Tahoe Basin, were affected by floods during January 1-3, 1997. In the two California counties surrounding Lake Tahoe, El Dorado and Placer, about $91 million in estimated damage was attributed to flood waters. Flooding in the Lake Tahoe Basin was mainly along the Upper Truckee River in the city of South Lake Tahoe.

    Through this analysis, the limitations on it are to make a classification of the visuals and extract the NDVI index only, for any satellite visuals, and this is not only limited to the floods in this area.
